# Responsible Social Media Use Checklist for College Students

A simple guide to help college students use social media safely, smartly, and responsibly.

## Do's

- **Do** maintain a clear distinction between your personal and academic/professional life.
- **Do** think twice before posting anything ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, family, or future employer saw this?"
- **Do** use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all social media accounts.
- **Do** respect others' privacy avoid tagging people without permission and do not share others' personal information.
- **Do** verify information before sharing help stop the spread of fake news and misinformation.
- **Do** take regular digital detox breaks to maintain good mental health and focus on studies.
- **Do** report cyberbullying, harassment, or inappropriate content immediately.

## Don'ts

- **Don't** post personal contact details, location, or sensitive photos publicly.
- **Don't** engage in arguments, hate speech, or cyberbullying even if you disagree with someone.
- **Don't** share exam papers, assignment answers, or confidential college information.
- **Don't** accept friend requests or messages from unknown people.
- **Don't** compare your life with others on social media, it can negatively affect your mental health.
- **Don't** post while angry or emotional, sleep on it before posting anything controversial.
- **Don't** overshare about your college life, attendance, or internal matters.

---

**Remember:**  
Your social media profile is a digital footprint. What you post today can impact your future opportunities.

**Stay Safe | Stay Smart | Stay Responsible**
