# Stay Safe Online Checklist

**A Practical Guide for College Students in India**

Follow these actionable tips to stay protected from cyber threats while using social media, UPI, and the internet.

## Essential Safety Tips

1. **Use Strong & Unique Passwords**  
   Create long passwords combining letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across accounts.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**  
   Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and banking accounts for an extra layer of security.

3. **Think Before Clicking**  
   Never click on suspicious links received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email. Verify the sender first.

4. **Verify Information Before Sharing**  
   Always check the authenticity of news or messages before forwarding them in college groups.

5. **Protect Your Personal Details**  
   Avoid posting or sharing your full address, college ID, Aadhaar number, or phone number publicly.

## Financial & UPI Safety Tips

6. **Never Share Your UPI PIN or OTP**  
   Treat your UPI PIN like your ATM PIN. Never share it with anyone, not even friends or customer care.

7. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Transactions**  
   Never do banking, UPI payments, or check your balance on college or public Wi-Fi. Use mobile data instead.

8. **Download Apps Only from Official Stores**  
   Install banking and UPI apps only from Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown sources.

9. **Set Low Daily Transaction Limits**  
   Keep your daily UPI and bank transfer limit low (₹5,000 – ₹10,000) for better safety.

## What to Do If You Are a Victim
```
* Report immediately on the **National Cyber Crime Portal**: [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* Call the **National Cyber Crime Helpline**: **1930** (available 24×7)
```

**Remember:** The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovering your money and stopping the fraudsters.
